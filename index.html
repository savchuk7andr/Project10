About us

The tiger is the heaviest of the modern wild cats, but its different subspecies vary greatly in weight . Large males of the Amur tiger can reach 3.5 meters in length without the tail and weigh more than 300 kilograms . However, on average, males of the Amur tiger subspecies reach 190–220 centimeters in length without the tail with an average weight of 227 kilograms (the tiger's tail is usually 60–110 cm long). The heaviest Bengal tiger , whose weight has been scientifically determined, weighed 389 kg. Females are usually noticeably smaller than males, in the Amur and Bengal subspecies they weigh 100–181 kg. Island tiger subspecies are smaller than mainland tigers , for example, the Sumatran tiger is on average much smaller than the nominate Bengal tiger subspecies : its average weight is 100–140 kg for males and 75–110 kg for females . [ 5 ] The extinct Bali tiger was even smaller: males weighed 90–100 kg, females 65–80 kg .
